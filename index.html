OpenFax

OpenFax is a study tool web application. It dynamically generates factsheets for an OpenStax textbook by pulling in information from a PostgreSQL database.

Currently, there are some 2000 factsheets available for the OpenStax Biology textbook. Each factsheet is composed of the term, definition, summary, picture, recommended resources, and related terms.

Term - a glossary term pulled from the OpenStax Biology textbook using a custom built parser.

Definition - a definition term pulled from the OpenStax Biology textbook using a parser.

Summary - Initially, the idea was to generate summaries for all the terms using the OpenStax textbook through NLP. However, this proved to be a bad idea due to insufficient data and difficulties of summarization. Eventually, just decided to web scrape data from Simple Wikipedia for summaries.

Picture - pulled from the OpenStax Biology textbook with the usage of the parser.

Recommended resources - generated web scraping. Currently only consists of YouTube videos relating to the term.

Related terms - Constructed a weighted graph with nodes as glossary terms, and the edges' weights being the number of times they appeared together within the same sentence or adjacent sentences. For each glossary term, linked to the 3 closest terms (highest weight between their edges).

EmpoweredPatient

Our motto is simple: "It's like having a doctor in the family." We provide (very) case specific questions for patients to ask their physician, tailored to their exact situation using experienced practitioner guidance. This helps empower them to make better treatment decisions. No longer will hidden financial, institutional and physician bias be the only forces in play during the critical decision making process.

Developing an Android/iOS app to provide case specific questions for patients to ask their physicians, tailored to their exact situation using experienced practitioner guidance.

UberBot

Developed for HackRice7.5

At Rice, there's a Facebook page Rice Students Ubering Together. When people want to find other people to Uber together with to the airport to save on some $$$, they would have to go to a spreadsheet located on the Facebook page, search for the destination, search for their time, and see if there were slots available (or if their desired time didn't exist, they had to make their own!)

But now, with UberBot, the whole process is automated. All you need to do is join our Slack Workspace and you can use commands for our bot!

You just tell Starter Bot a command and it will automatically put you into a private channel with other people that are going to the same airport on the same date and time!
